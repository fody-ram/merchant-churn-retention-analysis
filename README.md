# 🛒 Merchant Churn & Retention Analysis

> A comprehensive Power BI project focused on understanding merchant churn, retention patterns, and business health.

---

## 📌 Executive Summary

This project analyzes **merchant churn and retention** using a large e-commerce transaction dataset (2009–2011) covering **5,881 unique merchants**.

**Key Highlights:**
- **50.79%** overall churn rate — more than half of all merchants stopped transacting
- Average merchant **tenure is only 9.1 months**
- Late 2011 cohorts showed dramatically better retention (**90–100%**)
- United Kingdom dominates total revenue, while Norway leads in average order value

---

## 🎯 Project Objectives

- Measure overall churn rate and retention performance
- Identify which onboarding cohorts perform best
- Analyze relationship between tenure and churn risk
- Understand country-level and value-based merchant segmentation
- Discover key business trends in revenue and transactions

## 🔑 Key Findings

- **High churn rate** (50.79%) indicates a significant retention challenge
- Most merchants churn early — average tenure is only **9.1 months**
- Late 2011 cohorts performed exceptionally well (90–100% retention)
- Strong **seasonal peak** every November
- High-value merchants are generally well retained

---

## 💡 Recommendations

1. Implement a structured **Early Onboarding Retention Program** (first 3–5 months)
2. Launch targeted **win-back campaigns** for high-spend churned merchants
3. Investigate and replicate the successful practices from late 2011
4. Prepare for seasonal demand spikes in November
